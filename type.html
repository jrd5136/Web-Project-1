Classical

Ancient Indians were intensely inspired by the spiritual power of music. It is based on ragas i.e. colors that provides base for performance by scales and harmonies. The form of music is based on ragas (melody) and talas (rhythm). The ragas are usually played on Sitar instrument. Carnatic and Hindustani are main class of classical music that can be either instrumental or vocal.

Hindustani Music is purely based on Raga System which consists of basic melodic scale known as Sa Re Ga Ma Pa Dha Ni. Melody is composed by ascending and descending movement of seven scales.

Carnatic music, one of the oldest form of music in the world is also called South Indian music, inculcated with emotion and spirit spontaneously. There are basically 7 rhythmic cycles and 72 fundamental ragas. It is more devotional type of music and uses more instruments than Hindustani music.

Folk

Folk music has become most integral part of Indian Music. Various regions have their own varieties of folk styles which made it more diverse. This can be performed in any occasions like birthday, wedding and engagement ceremony. All the states in India recognized with their Folk music which distinguished culture and show rich heritage. Few types of the Folk music are RabindraSangeet, Bihu, Garba, Ganasangeet, Uttarkhandi, Lavani, Rajasthani and Bhangra.

Garba a style of Music as well as dance form in the state of Gujrat, India. Garba often accompanied with dhol (drum) and shehnai (reed instrument). Another most popular folk music is Bhangra. It’s a traditional music and dance form in the state of Punjab, India. Bhangra often perform to celebrate spring and a festival called “vaishakhi” in harvest season. Actual meaning of Bhangra is “a person intoxicated with joy” which really inspires even those who don’t sing or dance to shake it all out.

Indi Pop

In early 1980, Once British band Monsoon had mixed up their music with Indian Music ‘Ragas’. That’s the first time the term “indi Pop’ was introduced as singer Sheila Chandra has composed a music with ‘Ragas’ and western instruments. Since 90s, Many Indian singers rose to popularity of Indi Pop throughout the world by mixing up western music with Ragas.
